Swift Playgrounds 4: Things we know so far

During WWDC 2021 keynote Craig Fedrighi teased Playgrounds 4 for iPad by saying that it will bring the ability to “build apps on iPad”. Later that day we saw a short demo of the new Playgrounds during “Platforms State of the Union”, a developer centric keynote that dives deeper into the newly announced tools and technologies. Since that day we haven’t heard anything more about the Playgrounds 4 update.

Here are a few things that we know so far:

  1. You will be using SwiftUI to build apps (for iPhone and iPad). So no UIKit.
  2. There would be a new project type that will also work on Xcode for Mac, allowing you you to switch between devices seamlessly.
  3. It will support improved code-completion.
  4. Access to a library of view components to give users a quick access to common views like Buttons, Labels etc.
  5. A guide for creating your first app, helpful for beginners.
  6. Ability to upload your app to App Store Connect directly from your iPad.

The official iPadOS 15’s page says “Available late 2021”, so we should expect it somewhere around November or December, however it won’t be surprising if we get a beta in October. High hopes.
